Anxiety & Overthinking

Your mind is trying to protect you. But sometimes protection becomes a prison. Let's calm it down together.

😰

Anxiety

Anxiety is your nervous system in high alert. The goal is not to eliminate it — it's to tell your body it's safe.

→

5-4-3-2-1 grounding: name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste

→

Place both feet flat on the floor. Feel the ground holding you. You are safe right now.

→

Box breathing: inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4 — repeat 4 times

→

Write every worry out of your head and onto paper — your brain relaxes when thoughts are external

→

Remind yourself: this feeling will pass. It always has before.

How to Stop Overthinking

Overthinking is not a thinking problem — it's an anxiety problem. Your mind loops because it feels unsafe to stop.

→

Set a "worry time" — 15 minutes where you're allowed to think about the problem, then stop

→

Ask: "Is this thought helpful right now or just familiar?" Familiar is not the same as true.

→

Do something with your hands — cook, clean, draw — your brain cannot deeply overthink while your hands are busy

→

Write the thought down, then write: "Even if this happens, I will handle it because..."

→

Notice the thought without engaging: "I'm having the thought that..." — you are not your thoughts
